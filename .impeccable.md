## Design Context

### Users
Players of a browser-based 4X strategy game. They use the interface to read the world state quickly, evaluate risk and opportunity, issue map-driven decisions without friction, and conduct diplomacy with AI-driven rival civilizations that should feel like distinct personalities rather than scripted NPCs.

### Brand Personality
Clean, immersive, modern.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark strategy-table presentation with restrained metallic accents, map-first composition, and game HUD density rather than dashboard-card sprawl. The provided `frontend/design.png` is the primary visual reference and should be followed closely in overall mood and structure, while still adapting to the actual game data and responsive behavior. Diplomacy should feel like an active intelligence and negotiation layer, not an afterthought.

### Design Principles
- The map is the hero surface; all supporting UI should frame decisions, not compete with the terrain.
- Information should feel layered and tactical: immediately scannable at a glance, richer on inspection.
- Styling should feel authored and game-like, with strong atmosphere and disciplined contrast instead of generic app UI patterns.
- Controls should feel deliberate and calm, with clear hierarchy between strategic status, selected entity details, and actionable commands.
- Rival leaders should feel present. Diplomatic threads, stance, and incoming messages should read like contact with living factions.
- The interface should stay legible and usable across large desktop layouts and smaller screens without breaking immersion.
